The Problem

No Definition.
The DoW never defines human lethality at the individual level. What is not defined cannot be measured. What is not measured cannot be developed. Every dollar spent on lethality development for America's 155,000+ close combat fighters rests on untested assumptions.

Commanders certify America's 155,000+ warfighters annually against a standard that never measures whether a single one of them is lethal in the close combat fight. Binary output. Valid twelve months. Three Structural Failures
Wrong Organ. Wrong Geometry.
Wrong Clock.
FAILURE 01
Wrong Organ
Weapons qualification assesses the prefrontal cortex under static conditions. Close combat requires basal ganglia-resident automaticity. The stress response correctly suppresses prefrontal function when the fight begins. The institution certifies the organ the body shuts down when it matters.
FAILURE 02
Wrong Geometry
The qualification target is nearly ten times larger by area than the anatomical zone that determines incapacitation. A kneecap hit and a cardiopulmonary zone hit score identically. When the Marine Corps introduced a qualification requiring hits on anatomically defined lethal zones, the expert rate fell from 65 percent to 20–30 percent. The shooters did not become worse. The measurement became honest.
FAILURE 03
Wrong Clock
TC 3-20.40 Appendix H encodes a 3-second engagement window at CQB distances. The mandatory qualification appendices governing the entire force provide 5 to 16 seconds at the same distances. The Army published the correct temporal standard and declined to apply it. Qualification certifies performance at three to five times the operational clock. Whatever it is measuring, it is not the close combat fight.
The Definition
Λ = CLI / SVI

Lethality is the capacity to produce incompatibility with continuance. At the human level: the ability to incapacitate a target within the imposed constraints. This definition forces exactly three domains — not two, not four. Three. This is not a modeling choice. It is a logical consequence of the definition.

C
Cognition
The fighter acquired the right target — automatically, without deliberation, below the threshold of conscious decision-making. Measured by the Decision Accuracy Score (DAS). Basal ganglia-resident at competency.
Cognition Domain  |  DAS
P
Precision
The fighter delivered an incapacitating hit. Precision is the expression of Cognition — the motor program executing accurately against the correctly identified object. Measured by Lethal Zone Hit Percentage (LZHP).
Precision Domain  |  LZHP
S
Speed
The fighter acted within the imposed constraints. Speed is not a trained capacity. It is the emergent output of Cognition and Precision reaching unconscious competency simultaneously. Measured by Normalized Speed Score (NSS).
Speed Domain  |  NSS

The Cycle
Measure. Diagnose.
Predict. Prescribe.
Every prior DoW human performance program stops at Measure. LethalOS closes the loop.
01 — MEASURE
Measure
Live-fire close-quarters instrumented session. Proprietary measurement algorithms executing in real time. CLI, CFI, SVI produced at individual and formation level. Where every prior program stops.
02 — DIAGNOSE
Diagnose
Domain deficit signature analysis. Four-quadrant diagnostic placement. The fighter who scores low on DAS is not slow — he is engaging the wrong target sequence. The distinction determines the intervention.
03 — PREDICT
Predict
CLI trajectory modeling. Performance degradation curves. Fatigue mapping. The commander knows where each fighter will be in 30, 60, 90 days — before the next engagement.
04 — PRESCRIBE
Prescribe
Individual-level intervention directives. Not a leaderboard. The Speed Emergence rule prohibits speed-focused work for fighters whose Cognition or Precision has not reached automaticity — because prescribing speed to an underdeveloped domain embeds error at pace.
