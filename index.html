Three Structural Failures
Wrong Organ. Wrong Geometry.
Wrong Clock.
FAILURE 01
Wrong Organ
Weapons qualification assesses the prefrontal cortex under static conditions. Close combat requires basal ganglia-resident automaticity. The stress response correctly suppresses prefrontal function when the fight begins. The institution certifies the organ the body shuts down when it matters.
FAILURE 02
Wrong Geometry
The qualification target is nearly ten times larger by area than the anatomical zone that determines incapacitation. A kneecap hit and a cardiopulmonary zone hit score identically. When the Marine Corps required hits on anatomically defined lethal zones, the expert rate fell from 65 percent to 20–30 percent. The shooters did not become worse. The measurement became honest.
FAILURE 03
Wrong Clock
TC 3-20.40 Appendix H encodes a 3-second engagement window at CQB distances. Mandatory qualification appendices governing the entire force provide 5 to 16 seconds at the same distances. The Army published the correct temporal standard and declined to apply it. Qualification certifies performance at three to five times the operational clock. Whatever it measures, it is not the close combat fight.
